Introduction
Photonics: Science of producing, controlling, and detecting photons.
Combines optics, electromagnetism, quantum mechanics.
Applications: Communication, data processing, transportation, medicine, biotechnology, lighting.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
⚡ Advantages of Photonic Devices over Electronic Devices Q. Mention the advantages of photonic devices over electronic devices.
High bandwidth → faster communication.
Lower power consumption → less heat, more efficiency.
Increased security → harder to intercept.
Higher reliability → less overheating, longer lifespan.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
🔦 Photodetectors Q. What is a photodetector? Give examples.
Device that converts light → electrical signal.
Requirements: High sensitivity, reliability, short response time, low bias voltage.
Examples: Photodiodes, Phototransistors.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
📍 Photodiodes Q. What is a photodiode?
Reverse-biased p-n junction that generates current proportional to light intensity.
Types: Junction, PIN, Avalanche, Schottky.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Junction Photodiode Q. Explain construction and working of a junction photodiode.
Construction: Reverse-biased p-n junction, active area with anti-reflection coating, depletion region absorbs photons.
Working: Incident photons create electron-hole pairs → photocurrent + dark current.
Applications: CCDs, smoke detectors, TVs, remote controls, optical communication.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PIN Photodiode Q. Explain the working of a PIN photodiode.
Structure: p-region, intrinsic region, n-region.
Intrinsic layer absorbs more photons → higher photocurrent.
Advantages: Better efficiency, speed, sensitivity.
Applications: RF/microwave switches, fibre optic networks, X-ray/gamma detection.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
☀️ Solar Cell Q. Explain the principle of a solar cell.
Works on photovoltaic effect: converts sunlight → electrical energy.
Q. Explain construction and working of a solar cell.
Construction: Thin n-layer, heavily doped p-n junction, anti-reflection coating.
Working: Sunlight excites electrons → flow through external circuit → current.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
I-V Characteristics
Voc: Open circuit voltage.
Isc: Short circuit current.
Pmax: Maximum power at specific load resistance.
Operated at Maximum Power Point (MPP).
Efficiency [ \eta = \frac{P_{max}}{\text{Input optical power}} ]
Fill Factor [ FF = \frac{P{max}}{V{oc} \cdot I_{sc}} ]
Range: 0.65 – 0.8.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Advantages
No fuel, no pollution, low maintenance.
Disadvantages
Brittle, large space needed, requires cleaning.
Applications
Satellites, defence equipment, rural electrification, calculators, watches, telecom systems, EV charging.
Stringing
Series: Voltage adds, current same.
Parallel: Current adds, voltage same.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Point source, moderate input power, fast switching.
